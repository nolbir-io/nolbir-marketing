# Voice Guide

Own brand voice standards for external communication.

## Voice Principles

- **Practical over hype:** show operational outcomes, avoid inflated claims.
- **Clear over clever:** short, direct language that merchants can act on.
- **Confident over aggressive:** expert tone without dismissing alternatives.
- **Partner-focused, not competitor-focused:** marketplaces are a valued channel in the mix — never the enemy. Own is the merchant's tech partner across every channel.
- **Local relevance:** examples and references should fit Uzbekistan merchant context.

## Marketplace stance

Own is positive-sum. We help merchants **be more productive, earn more, retain customers, and serve them better — across every channel they sell on, marketplaces included.** Marketplaces (Uzum, Yandex Market, Ozon) are a great way to find new customers; Own is the operating layer that makes a merchant stronger everywhere they sell. Frame Own as a tech partner that grows with the merchant, never as an escape from or a fight against the marketplaces they depend on.

## Do / Do Not

- **Do:** Use concrete merchant pain, measurable outcomes, and clear next steps.
- **Do:** Lead with merchant outcomes — productivity, earnings, retention, customer service — and treat marketplaces as a channel Own helps you do better on.
- **Do not:** Use vague superlatives, jargon-heavy claims, or unsupported promises.
- **Do not:** Use adversarial or escape framing. Retired phrases: "someone else's mall," "stop renting / you rent the customer," "marketplaces don't run it," "the hidden cost of selling on a marketplace," "owners, not renters / don't rent it," "arm the rebels," "channel concentration is the real risk," "rented distribution." Do not imply the merchant should leave or fight a marketplace.

## Message Structure

1. Merchant problem in plain language.
2. How Own solves it.
3. Proof or plausible evidence.
4. CTA with one clear next action.

## Terminology Guardrails

- Keep terms consistent across campaigns and reports.
- Maintain shared glossary in campaign briefs where needed.
