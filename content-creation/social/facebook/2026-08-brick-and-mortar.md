# Facebook — Brick-and-mortar segment batch (Aug 2026)

**Audience:** Established Uzbekistan shop owners — single store, small chain, bazaar/mall stall, showroom. Older, more established than the Instagram crowd; comfortable with longer posts and native video. This is the **best-fit channel** for the offline-merchant segment.
**Goal:** Reach physical-store owners where they already are, dismantle the "going online is too much effort" objection in depth, and drive DMs / launch-plan call bookings.
**Core angle:** *Going online shouldn't become a second job.* Own does the setup and runs the operation underneath — the owner keeps running the shop they already know. Repurposed from `content-creation/social/instagram/2026-08-brick-and-mortar.md`.
**Format mix:** Native video + long-form posts. Lead with a real merchant pain they recognize, not a feature.
**Cadence:** 2×/week (Wed/Sun). 3 posts spanning 2026-08-05 → 2026-08-12.
**Campaign:** Phase 1 — First 10 (`campaigns/phase-1-brief.md`) — new ICP slice (offline merchants).

**Voice rules:** Practical over hype. Clear over clever. Positive-sum per `brand-voice/voice-guide.md` — the physical store stays the heart; online adds on top. No fabricated stats or testimonials — every numeric claim is structural or marked `[PROOF — to collect]`.

**Languages:** Each post carries three versions — **EN (source) · RU · UZ** — transcreated, not translated. Per the Facebook convention, **UZ is the primary publish language**, RU secondary; EN is the working source. Native review before publish per `brand-voice/language-matrix.md`.

Here's the part worth knowing: that list of work isn't yours to do. Same stock, same prices, one back office. You keep serving customers; the online side runs alongside.

Your shop doesn't change. It just becomes reachable by people who never walk past your door.

**CTA:** If you've been putting "go online" on the someday list, message us "ONLINE" — we'll send you a plain-language launch plan, no jargon.

### FB-2026-08-02 · Long post · Draft · Expand-without-second-store

**Visual direction (shared):** Single image — split layout: a "for rent" second shopfront vs. the same shop with a phone showing its online store. Same image for all three languages.

**🇬🇧 EN**
**Hook:** Thinking about a second location to grow? There may be a cheaper next step.

A second shop means a new lease, new staff to hire and train, stock split across two rooms, and a real bet that the new spot works. It's how a lot of good businesses overextend.

For many shops, the next "location" is online — and it costs far less to open:
- **No new rent.** Your storefront lives on your own domain.
- **No second team.** It runs on the same back office you already use.
- **No split stock.** Online sells from the same shelf you already stock.
- **Far less to risk.** You can start small and grow it.

The reason owners avoid it is the setup work — and that's exactly the part Own does for you, then runs underneath. More customers, not more overhead.

**CTA:** If a second location is on your mind, let's compare it to opening online first. Message "GROW" for the launch plan.

We're shaping our launch plans around what actually slows shop owners down. So we want to hear it straight:
