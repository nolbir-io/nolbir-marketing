**The catalog-order sync problem.** On large platforms, the catalog (what you list) and the order layer (what you sell) are often loosely coupled. Works fine at 100 orders. In Uzbekistan — where the local rails are real but still maturing — that assumption is expensive. Own isolates each integration (Humo, Uzcard, BTS, OFD) so a provider change doesn't cascade into the rest of the stack.

**The ship-before-talking problem.** The most common failure mode in emerging-market commerce infrastructure isn't underfunding. It's building for two years before testing with a real merchant. We run design partners from week one. The roadmap is shaped by what blocks transacting, not what looks good in a demo.
