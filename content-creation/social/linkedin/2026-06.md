- Treats every channel as a configuration, not a re-platform project.

We're building that. It's called Own.

Marketplaces sell for you. Own runs your commerce — across every channel.

### LI-2026-06-02 · Long-form · Draft · Why now
Publish: 2026-06-16
Campaign: Phase 1 — First 10

**Hook:**
Three things had to be true at the same time for Uzbekistan commerce infrastructure to be a venture-scale opportunity. They are, now.

**Body:**
**1. Merchants are ready to grow beyond a single channel.**
Established sellers want direct channels, a customer relationship they can build on, and total sales growth — alongside the marketplaces that still drive their volume. What used to be a someday question is now a monthly planning conversation.

**2. The local rails are mature enough to integrate.**
Humo and Uzcard acquiring, BTS Express and Yandex Delivery, BNPL providers, OFD fiscalization, data-residency law. Five years ago this stack didn't exist coherently. Today it does — but no one has wired it into a single commerce platform.

**3. Global platforms don't fit.**
Shopify, BigCommerce, Magento — none ship with Uzbekistan rails. The integration burden falls on every merchant individually. That's a multi-month, multi-vendor problem repeated thousands of times across the market.

A modular, headless platform extended with the Uzbekistan layer turns that one-off integration burden into one platform decision.

That's the wedge. That's why now.

### LI-2026-06-04 · Long-form · Draft · Category framing
Publish: 2026-06-30
Campaign: Phase 1 — First 10

**Hook:**
Investors keep asking which existing category Own slots into. The honest answer is: none of them, on purpose.

**Body:**
**It's not a marketplace.** Marketplaces help merchants sell; Own helps merchants run the whole operation. Own keeps marketplaces in the channel mix — and adds the direct channels merchants own.

**It's not a global SaaS platform.** Shopify and BigCommerce don't ship with Humo or Uzcard acquiring, BTS or Yandex delivery, OFD fiscalization, in-country data residency, or Telegram as a sales channel. Months of integration work per gap, per merchant.

**It's not a point solution.** A payments wrapper. A delivery integrator. A catalog tool. Each solves one problem; none solve "did we actually launch."

**It's not an agency build.** Custom-fit in theory. Long timelines, high cost, vendor lock-in, no clear handover in practice.

Own is the operating system for Uzbekistan commerce. One platform. One owner. The local rails wired in. The merchant stops being the integrator.

Most categories that look "new" are actually old categories with a missing piece. The missing piece here is the country layer.

### LI-2026-06-05 · Long-form · Draft · Optimization metric
Publish: 2026-07-07
Campaign: Phase 1 — First 10

**Hook:**
Most pre-seed commerce companies optimize for signups. We measure three things: **Signed. Live. Transacting.**

**Body:**
*Signed* — scope agreed with the merchant. Channels, dates, owners written down.
*Live* — orders flowing across at least the direct channels (storefront, Telegram). Marketplace sync follows the roadmap.
*Transacting* — real orders, across channels, repeatedly. Reorders. Real revenue.

Why this matters: in emerging-market commerce infrastructure, the failure mode isn't acquisition — it's activation. Merchants sign contracts that never go live. Platforms ship features no one uses. Investors look at top-of-funnel growth that translates to nothing.

We're building a company whose unit of progress is *transacting merchants*, not signups. Every funding decision, hiring decision, and roadmap decision is gated on that.

For investors who back this stage and this geography: I'd welcome conversations with funds and angels who think in terms of compounding infrastructure, not paid-growth funnels.
