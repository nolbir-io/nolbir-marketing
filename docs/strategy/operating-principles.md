# Operating Principles — Borrowing from Apple, Google, and Shopify

How Own operates at a world-class standard in Uzbekistan by adopting the *principles* of the companies that built category-defining platforms — not their tactics, and not their geography.

> **Status:** Strategy note. Companion to the [playbook](../playbook/README.md). Informs positioning in [`campaigns/`](../../campaigns/) and creative in [`content-creation/`](../../content-creation/).

## The premise (and the sharpening)

The starting belief: *the only difference between us and them is people and location, and we have a team that can operate at their level.*

That is mostly right — with one correction that becomes the whole strategy:

- **What's portable:** their operating principles and the *standard* they refused to lower. A talented team in Tashkent can hold the same bar.
- **What's not portable:** a giant home market and a decade of cheap capital. We can't copy those.
- **The move:** don't copy their *tactics* — copy their *level*, and substitute their structural advantages with the one unfair advantage they can never have — **local depth and trust in Uzbekistan.**

The trap is imitating their output (a beautiful storefront, a big ad budget). The win is adopting their standards and pointing them at the one market where *we*, not they, hold the advantage.

---

## 1) Apple — the whole widget, crafted

**Principle:** Control the full stack so the experience is seamless. Start from the customer experience and work backward to the technology. Focus ruthlessly — say no to a thousand things.

**Applied to Own:**

- Own is **not** a thin SaaS layer stitched from a dozen plugins. The *integration is the product*: storefront, Telegram, Humo/Uzcard/Payme/Click payments, BTS & Yandex delivery, fiscalization, and data residency as one designed experience.
- The standard: a merchant goes **signed → live with zero "now go find a gateway" moments.**
- **Focus:** make 10 design partners flawless before chasing 100. Apple shipped one phone, not nine.
- **Win on "it just works," not on price.** Don't race local point solutions to the bottom.

**Gift to the play:** *craft as a moat.* In a market full of duct-taped tools, a product that feels inevitable is a real, hard-to-copy differentiator.

---

## 2) Google — 10x on one job, then own the default

**Principle:** Do one job 10x better, give it away to reach ubiquity, become the default everyone passes through, and let a data flywheel compound.

**Applied to Own:**

- **Pick one job and be 10x better:** *time-to-first-transaction across channels.* Make "signed → live → transacting faster than anyone in the country" the number we obsess and beat. That's our "search."
- **Distribution via defaults and partners:** embed with what merchants already touch — bank/card processors behind Humo & Uzcard, Payme/Click, BTS, Yandex Delivery, even the marketplaces. Be **the rails behind the rails.**
- **The data flywheel is the real moat:** every merchant we onboard teaches us how Uzbek commerce actually breaks — which payment fails, where fiscalization trips, what kills local conversion. That compounding operational knowledge is something **no foreign entrant can buy.** Instrument everything; let it compound.

**Gift to the play:** *distribution + a data flywheel that strengthens the longer we operate locally.* Foreign giants start at zero on local data; we start ahead and pull away.

---

## 3) Shopify — arm the rebels, align the incentives

**Principle:** Mission + incentive alignment. "Arm the rebels" against the marketplace giant, and price so you only win when the merchant wins. Open a platform so others build what you don't. Land simple, expand surfaces.

**Applied to Own:**

- **Make it a movement, not a tool:** "Help Uzbek merchants own their commerce instead of renting it from marketplaces." People follow movements, not feature lists — which is exactly how the brand-awareness / follower goal actually gets hit.
- **Align price with merchant success:** a GMV/transaction-linked component means Own earns when the merchant earns. In a low-trust market, that alignment *is* the pitch.
- **Open the platform:** let local devs and agencies build on Own (we're already headless/modular). That partner economy is both product surface *and* Google-style distribution.
- **Land, then expand:** storefront → payments → operations → (later) Capital-style financing. Credit is scarce here; once we can see merchant cash flow, financing is enormous.

**Gift to the play:** *a mission that recruits believers, and an incentive model that earns trust.*

---

## The synthesis — "the Own play"

> **Apple's integrated craft × Google's default distribution + data flywheel × Shopify's mission and aligned incentives — all built on a local moat (rails + compliance + trust + operational data) that a foreign giant structurally cannot replicate fast.**

| Borrowed from | Principle | Concrete Own move |
|---|---|---|
| Apple | Whole-product, crafted | One integrated commerce OS, zero rough edges, signed→live with no gaps |
| Apple | Focus / say no | 10 flawless design partners before 100 |
| Google | 10x on one job | Own "time-to-first-transaction" as the category metric |
| Google | Default distribution | Embed with banks, payments, logistics, marketplaces — be the rails behind the rails |
| Google | Data flywheel | Compound local operational data nobody else has |
| Shopify | Arm the rebels | Run a merchant *movement*, not feature ads |
| Shopify | Aligned incentives | GMV-linked pricing; win only when merchants win |
| Shopify | Ecosystem + expand | Open APIs for local partners; add Capital-style financing later |

## What this changes right now

Not philosophy for its own sake — it redirects current execution:

1. **The awareness campaign becomes a movement.** Reframe "own your commerce" from a product claim into a rallying identity for Uzbek merchants ("Owners, not renters"). Implemented in [`content-creation/paid-ads/2026-06-brand-awareness.md`](../../content-creation/paid-ads/2026-06-brand-awareness.md) as the lead angle.
2. **Publish a 10x promise** — a public signed→live SLA. Apple-level standard *and* a Google-style 10x claim in one.
3. **Start the partner/ecosystem motion in parallel with Phase 1**, not after. Distribution is built early, the way Google seeded Android.
4. **Hold an Apple bar on the first 10.** Every rough edge tolerated now becomes the standard we scale.
5. **Make pricing merchant-aligned** so trust is structural, not promised.
